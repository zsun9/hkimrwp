Interest Rate Differentials Under an Exchange Rate Convertibility Zone: A Carry Trade Perspective

HKIMR Working Paper No. 201916

By Joseph Fung and Eric Lam

This study is motivated by the negative HKD-USD interest rate diﬀerentials observed after the US interest rate hike on December 17, 2015. We ﬁrst analyse two practical concerns that are typical from the perspective of a carry trader: (1) the diﬀerence in borrowing rate and lending rate of a currency, and (2) the exchange rate loss perceived from prevailing HKD/USD market condition using a truncated distribution that reﬂects full conﬁdence in the HKD/USD Convertibility Zone un-der the Linked Exchange Rate System (LERS). We ﬁnd that these considerations largely rationalize the observed interest rate diﬀerentials. We then perform robust Bayesian statistical inference on the negatively skewed eﬀective carry-to-risk ratios. We ﬁnd that the most probable and the typical eﬀective carry-to-risk ratios are economically small. Our results are consistent with the Hong Kong Currency Board’s intrinsic stabilizing mechanism functioning eﬃciently.
