Quantitative Easing

HKIMR Working Paper No. 201908

By Wei Cui and Vincent Sterk

Is Quantitative Easing (QE) an eﬀective substitute for conventional monetary policy? We study this question using a heterogeneous-agents model with nominal rigidities, as well as liquid and partially liquid wealth. The direct eﬀect of QE is determined by the diﬀerence in marginal propensities to consume out of the two types of wealth, which is large according to empirical studies. QE therefore emerges as a powerful policy instrument to anchor expectations and stabilize output and inﬂation. However, QE interventions come with strong side eﬀects on inequality and welfare. Estimation of the model reveals that QE greatly dampened the U.S. Great Recession.
